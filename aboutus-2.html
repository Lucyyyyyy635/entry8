Times Square: Anger in the Chaos

Times Square, the so-called "Crossroads of the World," can be overwhelming. The crushing crowds, the lingering stench of garbage, and the unexpected outbursts of strangers shouting into the void—it's enough to ignite a spark of anger in anyone. Here, anger feels justified. It's a reaction to the lack of space, the noise that never fades, and the chaos that refuses to slow down. Yet, amidst this frustration, Times Square is a reminder of the raw, unfiltered energy of city life—intense, unpredictable, and, at times, infuriating.
